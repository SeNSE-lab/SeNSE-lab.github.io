Histology is also really easy for us!

  • Oct 10, 2019
  • Histology, Barrels

This picture is of a tangential slice of a mouse's right cortex and the darkened regions show the cortical barrels that each represent one individual whisker. These barrels have only recently (since the 1970s) been the subject of scientific study because they didn't show up well in conventional brain slices; they are parallel to the natural curvature of the brain. Before these structures were discovered, most brain slices were done at right angles, and it was only by rotating the axis of slicing that these barrels were discovered. Science asks us to think creatively to make new discoveries. Sometimes the path forward isn't clear, but sometimes the path forward is a simple as a little rotation.
